Hungry Shark Simulator

Computer Science Principles

During the creative process of designing and creating the game, there were several useful tools and resources that helped make our game possible such as other Scratch games, previous experience with different types of games, and what we learned with how code(s) interacts with each other. As Rishi and I looked on Scratch for inspiration to start our game we came across a very barebones game called “Catch Food”, which as the name implies was a game that involved the player moving a bowl to catch food that fell out of the sky. This game was very useful to us because it showed us how to write the code such that our shark sprite would eat the food that fell and it helped tremendously with creating the final version of our game. Previous experience with other games, namely platformers, helped since it gave us an idea of how difficult it would be to create and program a certain type of game. This is why we decided to choose to create the sharkfeeder game over a platformer maze type game, as it was more suitable with the time constraints and our limited knowledge with code.
